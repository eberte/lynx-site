Eurasian Lynx tend to be between 80 to 130 cm length, weighing around 18 to 40kgs. Eurasian Lynx are fast predators, mainly due to their long legs and huge webbed paws. Lifestyle

Eurasian Lynx are mainly found in dense forests, but also are commonly found on rocky areas such as mountain peaks or inland cliffs. Though very rare, Eurasian Lynx can also be found in desert and humid areas.

Population

Eurasian Lynx are mainly found in Europe, parts of the Middle East and Asia, and stretch as far as the Tibetan Plateau. However, Eurasian Lynx population in Western Europe are now fragmented and very small due to various threats. The population worldwide is 50,000, the majority of them in China and Russia.
Eurasian Lynx are primarily threatened by illegal fur hunting, but also die from habitat loss and lack of prey species. Natural threats are few to Eurasian Lynx, though other animals such as wolves will opportunistically kill them.
Eurasian Lynx are solitary besides mating season, as most cats are, meaning the only long lasting relationship Euran Lynx truly develop is between mother and cub. However, male and female communicate through scent marks by overlapping their territories, which is how they are able to mate. Breeding season focuses on February and March, which is typical of cold weather cats which have their cubs in time for spring and summer so that they properly adapt and grow strong before winter arrives. Female Eurasian Lynx typically birth 2-3 kittens at a time. They are fully weaned within 6 months after birth and become completely independent after 10 months. In captivity Eurasian Lynx can live up to their 20s, but typically die in their teens in the wild.
