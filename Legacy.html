Legacy

History

Lynx occupy much of what is now the North american, Asian, and European Continents. The ancestor of the modern species Lynx is the Issiodorensis, which resided in Europe. Lynx are a part of the Mammalia class in the Felidae family. Lynx are strictly carnivorous (only eat meat).

Compare/ Contrast to Other Cats

Lynx(50-70 cm) are a relatively large cat, similar in size to the leopard(60-70 cm) and jaguar(63-76 cm), just smaller than cougars(60-90 cm) and cheetahs(70-90 cm). The average house cat has a top speed of 29.8 mph, the lion : 49.7 mph, the cheetah : 74.6 mph, and the lynx : 50 mph. The average house cat can jump eight feet high, while the lynx only jumps around 6.5 ft.
